The Nation of Islam and Black Muslim Identity

In the twentieth century, the Nation of Islam became one of the most visible Islamic movements in America. Its message of discipline, self-respect, racial uplift, and economic independence gave many Black Americans a framework for dignity and reform.

Even though its theology differed from mainstream Sunni Islam, its role in shaping Muslim identity, public visibility, and communal transformation in America was historically significant.
